The Beginning

A former musician who refused to quit.

Kevin Moll spent years as a recording engineer and home audio installation specialist before he did what every audio obsessive dreams of: opened a shop dedicated entirely to the pursuit of great sound.

In 2016, Skylabs Audio opened its doors in Valley Junction — a historic shopping district in West Des Moines that still feels like the kind of place where things are made with care and sold with passion.

The mission was simple: recreate the stereo shopping experience from the golden era of hi-fi. Help people of all ages find their own blend of vintage charm and modern performance. Make the experience of buying a turntable feel the way it did in 1974.

The YouTube Chapter

180K subscribers and still learning.

What started as a way to share repair tips with local customers became one of the most trusted vintage hi-fi channels on YouTube. Kevin publishes weekly — no scripts, no sponsors that compromise his opinions, no recommendations he wouldn't make in his own shop.

Two videos crossed one million views. Not because of algorithms or thumbnails — because they told the truth about what's worth buying and what isn't. That kind of trust takes years to build. Skylabs has been building it since day one.

The Online Store

Bringing Iowa's best to the world.

In 2023, Skylabs launched its online store — not to replace the in-store experience, but to extend it. The same careful curation, the same brands Kevin trusts, the same honest advice — now available to audiophiles in Des Moines and Düsseldorf alike.

Every product on this site is one Kevin has personally used, tested, or recommended to customers in his shop. Nothing goes online that wouldn't go into his listening room.

The Journey

A decade of sound.

2016

Kevin opens Skylabs Audio in Valley Junction, West Des Moines. Six turntables, twenty vintage receivers, and an unreasonable amount of passion.

2018

The YouTube channel launches. First video: how to clean a vintage turntable. Nobody expects it to go anywhere. Kevin films it anyway.

2021

50,000 YouTube subscribers. Kevin expands the shop, adds new partnerships with Rega, Wharfedale, and Grado. The community finds its voice.

2023

Skylabs launches its online store. New authorized dealerships: Cambridge Audio, TEAC, Wharfedale, Audio Technica, Advance Paris.

2024

89,200 subscribers. Two videos cross one million views. The Skylabs Podcast launches. The obsession, it turns out, is contagious.
