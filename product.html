Overview

Rega
Planar 3

The Rega Planar 3 is not a turntable. It is a statement. For over four decades, it has sat at the centre of serious listening rooms around the world — the benchmark against which all other turntables at its price point are measured.

Hand-assembled by a small team of dedicated technicians in Southend-on-Sea, England. Each unit leaves the factory with the same care that its creators would give their own listening systems.

Craftsmanship

Built by hand.
Born in England.

The Planar 3's phenolic resin platter — Rega calls it the RB330 — is a work of engineering poetry. Cast, machined, and balanced to tolerances that most manufacturers simply don't bother with, it rotates with a stillness that you can feel in the music.

The tonearm is the legendary RB330 — a continuous cast design with zero play in any direction. It's the reason why cartridges track your records rather than dragging through them. The difference is something you hear immediately.

The plinth is hand-formed from high-density polymer foam sandwiched between two skins. It's light. Deliberately so. Less mass means less resonance. Less resonance means more music.
